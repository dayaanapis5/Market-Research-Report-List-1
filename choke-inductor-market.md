<p><ul><li>Self-Inductor</li><li>Mutual Inductor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The choke inductor market comprises two main types: self-inductors and mutual inductors. Self-inductors, or single-inductor components, store energy in their magnetic field when an electric current flows through them, primarily used for filtering and energy storage applications. In contrast, mutual inductors consist of two or more coils that transfer energy between themselves through mutual magnetic coupling. This design is commonly applied in transformers and inductive coupling applications, enabling efficient power transfer and signal modulation in various electronic devices.</p></p>
