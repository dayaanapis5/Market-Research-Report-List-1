<p><p>The automotive multimeter market comprises two primary types: digital and analog. Digital multimeters offer precise readings with enhanced functionality, including features like auto-ranging and data storage. They are favored for their ease of use and clarity in displaying measurements. In contrast, analog multimeters use a needle to indicate readings on a dial, providing a more traditional approach. Although less common today, they are still valued for their simplicity and ability to visualize fluctuations in measurements. Both types serve essential roles in automotive diagnostics.</p></p>
