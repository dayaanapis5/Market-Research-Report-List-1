<p><p>The Cancer Stem Cell Therapy market encompasses various transplant types. Autologous stem cell transplants involve using a patient’s own stem cells, minimizing rejection risks. Allogeneic transplants use stem cells from a donor, offering the potential for a broader range of treatment options. Syngeneic transplants use stem cells from an identical twin, providing a perfect genetic match. Other options may include umbilical cord blood or engineered stem cells, broadening therapeutic possibilities in cancer treatment and addressing diverse patient needs.</p></p>
