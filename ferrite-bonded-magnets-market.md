<p><p>Ferrite bonded magnets are categorized into two main types: injected magnets and pressed magnets. Injected magnets are produced through a process that involves mixing ferrite powder with a polymer and injecting it into molds, allowing for complex shapes and high precision. Pressed magnets, on the other hand, are made by compressing ferrite powder in a die under high pressure, resulting in denser magnets. Both types offer advantages in cost-effectiveness and performance, catering to diverse applications in industries like automotive and electronics.</p></p>
