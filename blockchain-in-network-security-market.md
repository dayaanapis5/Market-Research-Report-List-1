<p><p>Blockchain in network security encompasses three primary market types: private, public, and consortium blockchains. Private blockchains are restricted to a single organization, ensuring data control and privacy. Public blockchains, open to all, enhance transparency and decentralization but may compromise security for sensitive information. Consortium blockchains involve a group of organizations, balancing the benefits of privacy and transparency while allowing controlled access. Each type addresses distinct security needs and operational requirements, making them suitable for different applications in network security.</p></p>
