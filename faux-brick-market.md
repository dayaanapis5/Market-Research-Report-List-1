<p><p>Faux brick markets encompass various types of brick production methods that mimic traditional brick aesthetics while offering different functional advantages. Dry pressed brick is made from clay that is compressed in molds, resulting in a dense and durable product ideal for walls. Extruded brick involves forcing clay through a die, creating a continuous shape that is cut to size, offering consistent dimensions and efficiency. Malleable brick, being softer and more pliable, allows for easier handling and shaping in creative installations, making it versatile for designs.</p></p>
